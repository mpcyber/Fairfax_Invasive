Ailanthus altissima · Lycorma delicatula

Disturb the ground. Tree of Heaven moves in.

Fairfax County has been building and expanding roads for decades. So has A. altissima. The two patterns are not unrelated.

Origin
W&OD Trail — Former Railroad Corridor
Primary Density Cluster

The W&OD Trail sits on top of the old Washington & Old Dominion Railroad bed, which ran across northern Fairfax until the line was abandoned in 1968. A. altissima was already here by then. Tree of Heaven thrives in exactly the conditions that infrastructure creates: compacted, nutrient-poor soil with full sun exposure and no competition from established vegetation. Every road widening, utility trench, and graded embankment is an open invitation. That original railroad population has been pushing outward ever since. It is now the largest Tree of Heaven cluster in the county.

Current Growth
I-66 Transform — Completed 2022
Western Corridor

The I-66 Transform Project was the largest road widening in recent Fairfax history, tearing up miles of roadside soil through the western corridor when it wrapped up in 2022. Two years later, the iNaturalist record shows a measurable density response along that axis. The railroad pattern is repeating itself on a much faster timeline.

Route 28 / Sully Road — Active
Secondary Cluster Emerging

Widening through Centreville and Chantilly is still ongoing, and a secondary cluster is already taking shape there. The Spotted Lanternfly numbers tell the fuller story: 2,290 SLF sightings in the county versus 640 Tree of Heaven records. The insect is moving faster than the host plant is being documented.

Projected Growth
Embark Richmond Highway — Under Construction
Southeastern Fairfax

The Embark Richmond Highway project is rebuilding Route 1 through southeastern Fairfax. New lanes, bus rapid transit, utility relocation across ten miles of corridor. The conditions are the same ones that produced the W&OD cluster: compacted disturbed soil, cleared edges, full sun, no established vegetation to compete with. Given what the data shows in northern and western Fairfax, southeastern Fairfax is likely to see new clusters emerge as construction progresses and disturbed ground goes unmanaged.
